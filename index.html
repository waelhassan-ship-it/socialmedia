How to Use This Test

Answer honestly — this is for you, not for anyone else. There are no "right" answers, but patterns in your responses reveal whether your beliefs are truly yours or were installed without your consent.

Scoring Guide

For each statement, rate yourself 0-3:

  • 0 = Never / Not at all
  • 1 = Rarely / Slightly
  • 2 = Sometimes / Moderately
  • 3 = Often / Strongly

The Uncomfortable Questions

After seeing your results, sit with these:

1. The Counterfactual

If you had been born into a different Lebanese family (different sect, different region, different class), would you hold the same political beliefs you hold today? If not, are those beliefs really yours?

2. The Mirror Test

Take any criticism you have of another Lebanese community. Does that criticism also apply to your own community at any point in history? If yes, why does it only bother you when they do it?

3. The Benefit Test

Who benefits from you believing what you believe? Is it you? Your community? Or a political leader who needs your loyalty?

4. The Source Test

Can you trace your strongest political belief to a moment of independent research and reflection? Or does it trace back to something you were told before age 15?

5. The Empathy Test

Can you articulate — genuinely, not as a caricature — why someone from an opposing community believes what they believe? Can you find anything legitimate in their fear or grievance?
